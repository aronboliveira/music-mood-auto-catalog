## Fictional-ScarletTide OST (7 tracks) — the Ethereal Correction

The largest franchise cluster this batch. A systematic pattern emerges: RO tracks had a
pervasive **{Ethereal, Nostalgic}** placeholder pair applied across the entire soundtrack
regardless of individual track character. This batch corrects that:

- **Ethereal stripped from 5 of 7** (50, 75, 103, 130, 199): the tracks aren't ethereal
  in the atmospheric/otherworldly sense — they're concrete MIDI-synth compositions with
  strong rhythmic identities. Ethereal was conflating "old game audio" with actual
  ethereal qualities.
- **Nostalgic stripped from 5 of 7** (75, 103, 130, plus Ground-Theme and Fictional-NeonJewel which
  share this pattern): same lazy "retro = nostalgic" shortcut seen in SSBU tracks.

The corrected profiles reveal the true diversity of the RO soundtrack:

- **Combat tracks** (75-Come-and-Get-it!, 103-Invisible-Invasion, 130-Dread-and-Bold):
  Aggressive/Chaotic/Defiant/Energetic/Frenzy — hard-driving battle music.
- **Exploration/ambient** (104-On-Your-Way-Back, 157-Disillusion): Contemplative/
  Meditative/Melancholic/Lonely — deep introspective Fictional-Kw-98dc0157ing.
- **Town/gathering** (199-Fishman's-Horizon, 50-Out-of-Curiosity): Danceful/Groovy/
  Whimsical/Spiritual — communal celebration music.

This three-tier split mirrors the RO game structure (combat, field travel, safe zones)
and was invisible when tracks all wore the same Ethereal+Nostalgic uniform.

## Emerging Pattern: the Ethereal/Nostalgic/Epic Overcorrection Campaign

Across batches 20–25, a clear macro-pattern has emerged: the original classifier
over-applied three "prestige" moods:

| Mood      | Typical misuse                            | Batches removing it    |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Epic      | Any orchestral/game OST                   | 20, 22, 23, 24, 25     |
| Nostalgic | Any retro/game/childhood-associated track | 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25 |
| Ethereal  | Any synthesised/electronic/ambient track  | 24, 25                 |

Ethereal is the newest member of this "placeholder prestige" trio. In batch 25 alone,
6 RO tracks lost Ethereal. The pattern: these moods _do_ have valid uses (Oath-to-Order
is genuinely Epic; Fictional-AmberShore Fictional-CobaltCanyon is genuinely Ethereal), but the
classifier applied them as genre markers rather than emotional descriptors.
